Immigration Rules

Key principles governing entry, stay, and movement of foreigners in the Republic of Albania, including references to Law No. 79/2021 “On foreigners” and related acts.

Law 79/2021DCM 858/2021

Entry and stay

Foreigners may enter and stay in Albania with a valid visa or visa exemption, residence permit, or other title provided by law. Overstaying or working without authorisation where required is an offence.

Visa categories

Short-stay (C), long-stay (D), airport transit (A), and special categories (e.g. border visa for crews) follow Schengen-aligned rules adapted to national law. Each type has specific validity, entries, and permitted activities.

Schengen & residence holders

Certain nationals may enter visa-free for tourism if they hold a valid multiple-entry Schengen visa used at least once in the area, or a valid residence permit in Schengen states, subject to conditions in the visa regime decision.

Refusal and removal

Border police may refuse entry if documents are insufficient or security grounds apply. A visa does not guarantee entry; the border officer takes the final decision at the crossing point.
