Apply

e-Visa is an online application system developed by the Albanian government to pre-screen travelers wishing to visit Albania.

Applicants have full access to the e-Visa application system. They must complete the e-Visa process before traveling to Albania by air, land or sea.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION BEFORE APPLICATION

  1. Payment for the visa is not refunded / reimbursed in cases of rejection of the application.
  2. Obtaining a visa is not a guarantee that the foreigner is allowed to enter the Republic of Albania.
  3. Visa applications must be completed online and ensuring that the applicant is outside the territory of the Republic of Albania after payment of the visa fee.
  4. During the visa application verification process, it is strictly forbidden for the applicant, the invitee, or any other subject to communicate with the responsible institutions, except when contacted by the relevant Diplomatic or Consular Mission.
  5. The evaluation process after payment is received lasts a minimum of 15 working days and for exceptional cases, can take up to 30 working days.
  6. In case of non-payment of the visa fee after 15 days, the application will change to "Not paid" status and will be automatically refused.
  7. Applicants with dual citizenship must select the nationality of the passport they will use for travel. If the country in the travel document registered with e-Visa is different from the document the person has during the trip, the e-Visa will be invalid.
  8. All documents uploaded must be translated in Albanian or otherwise in the English language and must be scanned and in pdf format.

The appeal against the decision to refuse or annul a visa must be made within 5 working days from receiving this notification as provided for in Law 79/2021 “On Foreigners”.

The authority responsible is the Directorate for Consular Affairs at the Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Albania.

All appeals must be made via the official email: visa@mfa.gov.al

For a successful appeal, you must ensure you deposit the following via email:

  • Documented evidence and other proof that support the appeal.
  • Detailed explanation why the visa should have been successful.
  • Subject heading must include: Visa Refusal Appeal – Name, Surname and Application Code.

If these criteria are not met, the appeal request will not be taken into consideration.

If your appeal is unsuccessful, you must reapply 3 months from the initial date of refusal by the e-Visa system and the refusal must be declared in any future applications.

Exemption of visa to enter and stay in the Republic of Albania for up to 90 days in 180 days

  1. Foreign citizens who have a valid, multiple-entry Schengen visa, which has been previously used in one of the Schengen states, or foreign citizens who have a valid permit of stay in one of the Schengen states.
  2. Foreign citizens who have a valid, multiple-entry US or UK visa, which has been previously used in the respective country of issuance, or have valid permit of stay in the US or UK.
  3. Citizens from Saudi Arabia, Oman, Qatar, Indonesia and Thailand can enter without a visa, only for tourism purpose for the period 15 April 2025 until 31 December 2025 with the document passport.
  4. Holders of documents for travel by EU member states: Alien's travel document; Travel document (Refugees, Geneva Convention); Travel document (Stateless persons); Travel document for person enjoying subsidiary protection.
Visa regime for foreign citizens

The visa regime for foreign citizens in the Republic of Albania is provided by Law No. 79/2021 “On foreigners” and by the Decision of the Council of Ministers No. 858, dated 29.12.2021, “On the definition of the criteria of procedures and documentation for entry, stay and treatment of foreigners in the Republic of Albania”.

Application phases

First phase

Visa application process is only accepted if it is completed online, and you must ensure that you are outside of the territory of the Republic of Albania after payment of the visa fee.

After the application is submitted:

  1. If it does not fulfil any of the criteria necessary for the required visa, the application is rejected according to the legal framework.
  2. If it has irregularities, it is turned back to the applicant requesting the correct documents to be uploaded according to the instructions sent.
  3. If it is correct, a notification is sent that the application has been accepted and a visa fee is requested, according to the cost and type of visa requested.

Visa fees for foreign citizens (Council of Ministers No. 329, dated 29.05.2024) can be found here. Visa fees for minors aged 6–12 are half the visa fees for adults. The visa fee does not apply to: cases of reciprocity; humanitarian cases; holders of diplomatic and service passports; children under the age of 6.

After payment

  1. If the visa is rejected/canceled: the applicant is informed via email with the legal arguments.
  2. If the visa is approved, the electronic visa is generated and sent by email. You must be in possession of the visa at the Border Crossing Point when entering and leaving Albania.
  3. The assessment process as a rule takes up to 15 working days and in exceptional cases can take up to 30 working days.
  4. The applicant is notified via email that the application is in the "evaluation process" stage.

Within 5 working days from the receipt of the decision, the interested foreigner has the right to appeal against the decision of refusal/annulment of the visa at visa@mfa.gov.al. The authority responsible is the Directorate for Consular Affairs at the Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs.
